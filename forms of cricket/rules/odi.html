ONE DAY Cricket Rules


General Rules:

  1. In a single innings, each of the bowlers can bowl a maximum of only one-fifth of the total overs i.e. 10 overs in a total of 50 overs.
  2. If a bowler ever delivers a ball overstepping the popping crease, it is a no-ball. The batting team gets one run for the ball and the next delivery is a free hit. In other words, in the next ball, the batsmen can only be dismissed through a run out.
  3. If either of the umpires believes that a team is wasting time, he can award a five-run penalty, at his own discretion.
  4. In a normal ONE DAY game, the interval lasts for 30 minutes. However, in a reduced-overs match, it can be cut minutes.
  5. If, and only if, each of the two teams has faced (or had the opportunity to face) five overs, will be a match deemed to have played.
  6. In each ONE DAY cricket match, one short-pitched ball is allowed per over.
  7. To calculate a team's net run rate, the average runs per over scored by that team is deducted from the average runs per over scored against it.
